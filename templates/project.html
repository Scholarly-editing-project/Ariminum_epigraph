Roman Epigraphy Project

Our Goal

In developing the Roman Epigraphy project We tried to offer new solution for digital epigraphy analysis. We decided to create a new modular structure to make our archive simpler for both the manager and the user. To achieve this goal We have adopted the strategy of structuring the archive in a geographical point of view.

The single city represents the module of our archive (as it was the module in ancient roman administration); and once the user have reached it inside the map He can deepen the geographical navigation within the city. Scrolling through the streets of the ancient city (rebuilt based on the appropriate archaeological bibliography) the user can find information that will help him to explore the place, for example the places of interest, monuments and street names. Finally, within the city users can find the epigraphic documents virtually repositioned in their exact original position.
